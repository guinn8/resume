About the job

Location: North York, ON - Hybrid

Contract Duration: 12 months with a possibility of extension

Working Hours: 9-5/M-F


Job Title: Software Analyst


About Us

We’re seeking a skilled Software Analyst to join our innovative team. In this role, you will contribute to the design, development, and testing of medium to high-complexity components of mission-critical software systems. Working closely with Senior Analysts or Technical Specialists, you’ll develop expertise in functional areas of our advanced software systems while adhering to industry-leading safety standards and design methodologies.


Key Responsibilities

Software Development:

    Design, code, debug, and test software for medium-complexity or critical components.
    Develop and execute integration tests using simulated or target hardware test environments.
    Chair design reviews for assigned software components

Requirements and Analysis:

    Contribute to architectural design, algorithm refinement, and functional/non-functional requirements documentation.
    Perform detailed impact analyses of changes across software components.

Problem Solving:

    Identify and resolve software defects, implementing effective solutions.
    Conduct field testing and provide on-site engineering support when needed.

Collaboration:

    Work alongside the Systems Engineering team to ensure seamless integration of software with system requirements.
    Propose scalable and sustainable solutions aligned with project phases and timelines.


Qualifications

    Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science, Engineering, or a related field.


Experience:

    Minimum 3 years of relevant experience in software development for medium to large-scale applications.
    Hands-on experience with embedded systems programming in C/C++.
    Proficiency in scripting languages like Perl or Ruby.
    Familiarity with Agile methodologies and tools such as Jira, Git, Eclipse, or ClearQuest.


Technical Skills:

    Knowledge of software development environments (e.g., Microsoft Visual Studio).
    Understanding of railway signaling techniques, including SelTrac CBTC (preferred).
    Intermediate expertise in modeling, simulation, and working with operating systems such as Windows, Linux, and Unix.
    Practical experience with requirements management tools like DOORS.


Desired Skills

    Strong analytical skills for diagnosing issues using field logs and failure analysis.
    Ability to work independently within a collaborative team setting.
    Knowledge of control theory (e.g., PID, Fuzzy Logic) or graph theory is a bonus.


Tools:

    Experience with source control systems and IDEs such as Git and ClearCase.
